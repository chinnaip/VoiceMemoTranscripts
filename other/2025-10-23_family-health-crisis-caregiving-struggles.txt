I want to talk about something that's been going on for more than 25 years now. Around 2000, my father-in-law had a spinal injury and was paralyzed. They did some surgery with metal rods on his back. He used to walk and everything was fine for a while, but then he couldn't walk anymore. His condition got worse. After about 10 to 12 years, he started experiencing a lot of pain. The doctors had to replace the metal rods, but despite proper physiotherapy and many treatments, nothing worked. He started taking a lot of painkillers for pain management, which impacted his liver. He also developed fluid buildup in his lungs. So there were multiple complications. His condition deteriorated further, and eventually, he had to be hospitalized. He was on a ventilator for a while. When we reached the hospital, things were critical. He went through a lot during those 24 years. Before the injury, he was a very hardworking person, physically strong, and could do many things.

Then, eight years ago, there was another accident. He suffered a head injury and was paralyzed almost completely on the right side. It took almost a year for any recovery. We came back to Bangalore primarily for his treatment. Almost every year since then, we've had close calls where we almost lost him, but we managed to save him somehow.

Now, the situation is very painful, especially for my mother-in-law. Her own health has deteriorated significantly from all the stress. He himself has become unhappy about his situation. He's feeling very self-pitying. I wanted to get him a helper to stop the burden on my mother-in-law. But the problem is he won't even drink water himself because it creates the urge to go to the bathroom, which is difficult for my mother-in-law to help with. So he's been resisting everything and taking more painkillers, which makes things worse.

Two weeks ago, I went to my hometown and came back on Sunday morning. My wife mentioned something about 25,000 rupees. We were asking her to send it to his father if he's comfortable with it, so we could hire some help so he won't feel that discomfort of burdening her for everything. But instead, he started resisting more and went into a suicidal mode. The only thing is he wouldn't do it himself. He was just trying to avoid everything.

Now my mother-in-law will probably come to Bangalore. This situation is really very hard.
