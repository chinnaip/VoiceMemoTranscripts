Okay, so regarding the position. I'm sure this aligns with what you've been doing. Let me explain. I lead the data team for one of the departments at Morgan. We handle end-to-end data management. It's a 20-member team spread across the globe—US, Europe, and India. The team functions as a horizontal unit within the department. Our domain is fraud detection and prevention, specifically payment fraud. This spans almost all business processes within the organization.

To detect fraud, we work closely with the fraud investigation team. They examine every aspect of the customer journey—from call center interactions to financial advisory services, account creation, account management, and transactions. Money movement transactions are particularly important. We also consider related factors like device information and geographic information when analyzing fraud patterns.

Our team is on the critical path of payment processing. Here's how it works: let's say I'm a Morgan customer and I need to send money to you. I can do this through various modes in India—UBA, RTGS, IMPS—or online transactions, or even go to a branch to make a payment. These methods are applicable not just in the US but across the globe. The majority of our work is US business, though my team also supports other non-EGO entities. However, in my current role, I focus on US business.

When you send money through us, the fraud systems are in the middle of payment processing. The payment processing engine sends the request to our fraud system. The fraud system screens it and processes the payment to determine if it's fraudulent. If it's flagged as potential fraud, we immediately block the transaction. It's similar to how it works in India. The transaction then gets investigated within minutes—it's very time-sensitive. A human investigator reviews it and decides whether it's actual fraud. If confirmed as fraud, the transaction is stopped. If it's not fraud, we release it.
